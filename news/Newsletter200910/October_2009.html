Version 0.9 Released


In August, we released Equalizer 0.9 which added major new scalability features, for example the revolutionary cross-segment load-balancing. You can find a list all new features, enhancements, optimizations and bug fixes in the release notes. A paperback book of the Programming and User Guide is available from Lulu.com.



Application Spotlight:

CAVE Rendering Framework



Developed at the Engineering and Information Technology de-partment at the Bern University of Applied Sciences, the CAVE Rendering Framework (video) provides all functionality needed to render OpenSceneGraph applications using Equalizer without further knowledge about parallel rendering. It is compatible with most Equalizer configurations and supports many of the OSG features.

 
 

To promote the usage and visibility of open source software in Switzerland, the Swiss Open Systems User Group awards a yearly price. This year, the jury selected Equalizer as the winner in the category ‘Business’.

We are excited that the jury honored our engagement in the open source community, and we would like to thank our users for the continued support and usage of Equalizer.

The liberal LGPL open source license of Equalizer gives commercial developers the right to use it free-of-charge, while at the same time protecting the investment due to the continuous development of the core framework.

Coincidentally, the first independent open source application based on Equalizer has been released lately (see on the left). This marks another milestone after a wide adoption by closed-source applications in the industrial design, art, medical and architecture markets.

We’re looking forward to new developers in many new areas, both for commercial and open source projects.
